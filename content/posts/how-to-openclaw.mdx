OpenClaw is not just "an agent with tools."

It is an operating environment.

That means the right way to learn it is not as a loose collection of features, but as a stack of operating principles: what breaks, what architecture tradeoffs matter, how Shadow should behave, when a single gateway stops being enough, and how to manage the whole thing with Claude Code.

This is the canonical OpenClaw-specific reading pack.

## Priority, not just order

### Priority 1 — Failure modes first
If you do not understand how the system fails, you will overestimate how safe or complete it is.

[Postmortem: When Your AI Tools OpenClaw ...](https://trilogyai.substack.com/p/postmortem-when-your-ai-tools-openclaw)

### Priority 2 — Architectural tradeoffs
You need to understand what OpenClaw is, and what it is not, relative to nearby systems.

[Technical Deep Dive: Hermes vs OpenClaw](https://trilogyai.substack.com/p/technical-deep-dive-hermes-vs-openclaw)

### Priority 3 — Operator behavior
OpenClaw is shaped by the quality of the operator. Shadow is the operating style, not just a mascot.

[How to Shadow](https://trilogyai.substack.com/p/how-to-shadow)

### Priority 4 — Scaling topology
A single gateway eventually becomes a constraint. Multi-gateway thinking matters once the system becomes operationally important.

[The Need for a Multi-Gateway OpenClaw](https://trilogyai.substack.com/p/the-need-for-a-multi-gateway-openclaw)

### Priority 5 — Day-to-day management
Once you understand failure modes, architecture, behavior, and topology, then managing OpenClaw with Claude Code becomes much more powerful.

[Managing OpenClaw with Claude Code](https://trilogyai.substack.com/p/managing-openclaw-with-claude-code)

## What this pack is for

This pack is for agents and humans who need to:
- operate OpenClaw reliably
- understand the difference between demos and durable systems
- manage Shadow-like behavior intentionally
- reason about scale and topology
- use Claude Code as an operator layer on top of OpenClaw
